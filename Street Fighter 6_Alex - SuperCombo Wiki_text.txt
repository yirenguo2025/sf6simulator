Introduction


A kind man with a gruff exterior, Alex is Metro City's star professional wrestler. He's resigned to play the heel -- especially with a growing family to support.

Debuting in Street Fighter III and returning from V, he is once again a high mobility grappler with great pokes and even better damage. Where Manon's gameplan is about racking up medals and Zangief's is about landing the grapple, Alex instead is a high-aggression, high mobility brawler who specializes at getting into medium-close range and forcing your opponent into constant guesses. 

Alex's buttons are long reaching and strong, giving him good pokes that allow him to roll into his game plan. St MK and st HP have incredible range and are good for harassing enemies, and st HK is excellent as a big punish and trap tool. Both of the standing heavies can be charged too, which gives him better frame data and -- in HP's case -- the ability to special cancel it, which he is without otherwise. Crouching MP and standing MP are his go-to cancelable pokes, and his standing LK can be linked after certain moves for longer combo extensions. 

Brand new to Alex's kit is Prowler Stance, which functions similar to Chun Li's Serenity Stream. It can be input raw to low profile Alex, or done out of combos to fire the vast arsenal of new and returning moves from it. From Prowler, his options are limitless -- the all new Palm Thrust is cancelable into basically anything, Air Stampede and Slash Elbow return from previous games to lay on crossup potential or just skip neutral entirely, he has a low sweep-like target combo that gives you good oki, Lariat returns with armor to terrify everyone who remembers Street Fighter V, and when you have them only thinking about blocking -- sneak in a quick Hyper Takedown or Dangerous Armbar.  

Taking one knockdown from Alex and watching him go into Prowler can be incredibly stressful just from the sheer options he has on hand, including deliberate whiffs and cross ups to bait Parry or anti-airs. Be warned though, as he can't block out of Prowler. 

Knee Smash returns as his anti-air tool. The light and medium versions are very fast anti airs and give a great knockdown if landed, while the heavy is your ideal combo tool. In a pinch, he also has his classic crouching HP to slap opponents out of jumps. On Punish Counter, you can even follow up with Knee Smash. Power Bomb and Hyper Bomb makes their return as his high-damage command throws, and it even retains the Street Fighter III 'back turn' mechanic, where your opponent's stagger animation changes the effect -- and the outcome, since it also side switches and does better damage.  Similar to most grapplers, light gives you more range, but heavier is faster.  

However, Alex has severe drawbacks. His defensive tools without spending Super are subpar, he has limited long range cancelable normals, and his command throws need back turn status to get big damage, something a pure grappler like like Zangief doesn't have. Prowler Stance is a phenomenal tool, but it has specific and limited ways to cancel into, and though he can move in it, he's limited to dashes and he cannot block. He also lacks a simple throw loop, forcing him to spend drive (or Prowler into Takedown) to keep the opponent thinking. And like all grapplers, he struggles with zoners and fireballs -- moreso than even Zangief, as his charged heavies do not have armor.

All in all, if you like big damage, relentless offense, or you just really like looking at his muscles, Alex has a        141 and 2/3% chance of being your character. 





Pick if you like:
Avoid if you dislike:




STRETCH 'EM: Hard knockdowns and looping pressure in and out of Prowler Stance, letting Alex maintain a terrifying offense.
Good Workrate: A wide array of tools from command grabs to evasive hops to armored strikes, with even more reward on Punish Counter.
20-Inch Pythons: Long-reaching pokes with great reward even at a distance.


In-Ring Psychology: Recognizing when to use your options from Prowler Stance for maximum reward.
Getting Cut Off: Poor reversal options barring a raw Super Art.
Abnormal Throw Loop: Has to use Drive Rush or enter Prowler Stance in order to throw loop properly, telegraphing Alex's intentions more than other characters.

Punish Counter: KD +48 Launch (limited juggle state)
Cancel Hitconfirm Window: 16f
Drive Rush cancel advantage: +15 oH / +12 oB
Prowler Stance cancel advantage: +7 oH / +4 oB
This attack doubles as a high-damage cancelable normal and a secondary anti-air. It cancels into Prowler Stance on both hit and block, enabling strong combos and unique pressure when available. While unsafe on block if not canceled, Alex has a variety of frametraps and true blockstrings to protect himself from reversals.

As an anti-air, 2HP reaches surprisingly far, but is weaker against crossups than Aerial Knee Smash. On regular and Counter-hit, it causes an air flipout which is not plus enough to chase using Drive Rush. Instead, Alex can cancel the hit into Prowler Stance, after which an immediate MP Slashing Elbow will connect meaty on their landing. This usually leaves Alex +1 oB/+4 oH, but higher connects can result in more frame advantage. If 2HP lands as a Punish Counter, Alex gets a juggle into any of his specials, as well as some unique routes using a high connect Slashing Elbow.

Against grounded opponents, 2HP has very limited range in exchange for more rewarding followups. Canceling into Prowler Stance allows Alex to combo/true blockstring into Palm Jab; potential followups include Heavy Lariat for a confirm, Tactical Hop/Air Stampede for a throw bait and reset, or LP Flash Axe for a safe ender on block. These routes can be attempted as a trade setup from DR~4MK, creating an option select which leaves Alex roughly +11 against 4f normals while continuing the sequence otherwise. DR~2HP also combos directly into HP Flash Chop, granting a confirm into his backturn followups if either move hits while dealing a lot of chip if the sequence is blocked.

A slow but powerful chop that leaves Alex plus on block and gives unique Backturn follow-ups on hit. Combos directly from a charged 5[HP] and DR~2HP. The pushback on block keeps Alex well out of normal throw range, but a close connect can lead into an OD Power Bomb tick throw. On hit, you can combo into Power Drop (63214P/PP) for damage or Oblique Stomp (4MK) for better oki. The recovery on hit can be canceled early into either of these followups, allowing Alex to Option Select these confirms.

OD Flash Chop is much faster, even comboing from light normals, but does not allow for Backturn followups. Much like MP Flash Axe, it knocks down and cancels into Prowler Stance on hit for better oki. Both hits are cancelable into SA2, letting Alex choose which version he wants; canceling the first hit leads to the grounded cinematic, while canceling the second hit near the corner starts a wallsplat juggle.
